## What Lyra does

Most blogs stall for the same reason: writing good posts consistently is hard, and nobody has time to do it every week. Lyra removes that bottleneck without taking the decision out of your hands.

She runs the whole pipeline. Topics move from discovered to released on their own. She writes, reviews her own work, and rewrites until a post clears the bar, then hands you a pull request. Nothing publishes until you hit merge.

Think of it as Claude pointed at your entire blog instead of one document.

## 🔁 The loop

```
discover  →  write  →  fact-check  →  open PR  →  you merge
```

1. **Discover.** Lyra researches what is trending and winnable in your focus area, then dedupes against your existing posts so she never cannibalizes what you already rank for.
2. **Write.** On connect she reads your repo and learns where posts live, your frontmatter, your slug rules, and how you sound. She writes the way your blog already writes.
3. **Fact-check.** She fetches and confirms every external link or drops it. Claims are checked against current sources. Broken links and stale stats are blockers, not footnotes.
4. **Score.** Each post gets an editorial score out of 10 across content, SEO, technical, readability, and linking. She leaves herself review comments and rewrites until it clears the bar.
5. **Open a PR.** Output lands as a labeled pull request with a matching banner, committed through a GitHub App. She tags you when it's ready.
6. **You merge.** You have the final word. Merge it, request a change, or close it.

## 👤 Why founders and startups use Lyra

One good post barely moves the needle. Dozens of them, published steadily in your voice and actually fact-checked, are how search traffic compounds. That is the growth curve most startups want and few have the time to build.

Lyra gives you that output without adding a content hire or a freelancer queue. You stop writing the blog and start reviewing it. The work happens on its own; the judgment stays with you.

- **No new headcount.** The pipeline runs itself. Your job shrinks to review and merge.
- **Accuracy you can stand behind.** Lyra verifies before she ships, so you are not publishing broken links or stale numbers under your name.
- **You keep control.** Nothing goes live without your merge, and you own every token of spend.

## 🛠️ Why developers like the workflow

Lyra lives where your blog already lives: your repo.

- **Real pull requests.** Output arrives as a labeled PR through a GitHub App, so it fits the review flow you already use. Diff it, comment on it, merge it.
- **Nothing auto-publishes.** She opens the PR and tags you. Merging is always a human action.
- **Bring your own key.** Your Anthropic key does the writing. It is encrypted at rest and never marked up, so cost stays fully in your control. A Gemini key for banners is optional.
- **Minutes to set up.** If you have a GitHub repo for your blog and an Anthropic API key, you install the GitHub App, pick the repo, and you are set.
